Wheat

Wheat is the second most important cereal crop of India after Rice and plays a vital role in the food and nutritional security of the country. Wheat has been cultivated since prehistoric times in the world. From all possible records, it seems that it's center of origin in South-Western Asia. It is believed that Aryans brought wheat grains to India and since then it has been grown in India. Major wheat-growing states in India are Uttar Pradesh, Punjab, Haryana, Madhya Pradesh, Rajasthan, Bihar, and Gujarat.
